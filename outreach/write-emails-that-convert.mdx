## Phase 2: Craft your message

This is where you turn a name on a list into a potential partner. Quality beats quantity every single time.

### Write a subject line that commands attention

Think of this as your headline. It should be concise, personal, and value-driven.

| Instead of... | Try something like... |
| --- | --- |
| "Investment Opportunity" | "Following [Their Portfolio Company] in the [Your Industry] Space" |
| "Startup Looking for Funding" | "25% MoM growth in a market your portfolio knows well" |
| "Quick Introduction" | "[Mutual Connection] suggested I reach out about [Specific Topic]" |

<Tip>
  Our platform provides data-driven suggestions and templates used by top-performing founders. Check out [Message Templates](/outreach/message-templates) for proven starting points.
</Tip>

### Structure an email that gets read

Write like a human, not a robot. Keep it short, respectful of their time, and focused on them.

<Steps>
  <Step title="The Hook">
    Start with a genuine, specific connection. Mention a recent investment they made or an article they wrote. This shows you've done your homework and aren't blasting a generic template.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The Pitch">
    In two sentences, state what you do and the unique signal you have — traction, team, or technology. Be specific. Numbers speak louder than adjectives.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The Call to Action">
    Be clear and confident. **"I'd welcome 15 minutes next week to show you how we're solving this"** is far more effective than "I'd love to connect sometime." Give them a reason and a timeframe.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Perfect your pitch deck attachment

Your email gets the deck opened; your deck gets the meeting. Ensure it's a clear, concise PDF. CapitalxAI's tools help you create pitch-deck links that you can track, so you know when an investor has viewed it.

## Phase 3: Execute with precision

Now it's time to send, but timing and infrastructure matter.

### Send at the right time

Data shows best practices, but our analytics can refine this for you. Generally, aim for **Tuesday through Thursday**, during business hours in the investor's timezone.

| Factor | Best practice |
| --- | --- |
| **Day of week** | Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday |
| **Time of day** | 8–10 AM in the investor's local timezone |
| **Avoid** | Monday mornings, Friday afternoons, weekends, and major holidays |

## Phase 4: Follow up with context, not just reminders

The first email is just the start. A disciplined follow-up process is what separates professionals from amateurs — but it's not just about sending another email. It's about building a bridge.

<Warning>
  A generic "just checking in" email is a missed opportunity. Your follow-up should deepen the connection by painting a clearer picture of why this specific investor is the right partner.
</Warning>

### Make every follow-up count

Let's say you're reaching out to a partner at a firm that led a round for a company in an adjacent space. Your follow-up could say:

```text Example follow-up
Hi [Name],

Following up on my note about [Your Startup]. Given your deep experience
with [Portfolio Company] and their work on [Specific Challenge], I was
particularly keen to get your perspective on how we're approaching
[Your Related Solution].

Your insight into scaling in this market would be invaluable.
```

This isn't a nudge. It's an invitation to a strategic conversation, showing you've done the work to understand where they can add unique value beyond capital.

</Note>
